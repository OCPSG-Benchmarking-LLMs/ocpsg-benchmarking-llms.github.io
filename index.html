OCPSG Benchmarking LLMs is a project website for benchmark development, documentation, and reproducible evaluation workflows for large language models and fine-tuned models applied to multilingual policy agenda annotation in parliamentary speeches.

The project is developed within the Oxford Computational Political Science Group (OCPSG), a research initiative supported by Oxford’s Department of Politics and International Relations. OCPSG advances the use of computational methods, including machine learning and AI, in political science and policy through an interdisciplinary and collaborative research environment.

What this site provides

This site serves as the public entry point for the benchmark and its supporting infrastructure. It is intended to make benchmark design, model evaluation, and submission workflows more transparent and easier to reproduce across research teams.

The benchmark infrastructure includes:

  • multilingual benchmark datasets for parliamentary speech classification;
  • standardised prediction and evaluation contracts;
  • model wrappers and submission templates;
  • reproducible scoring workflows and reporting conventions;
  • documentation for benchmark use, outputs, and submissions.

Current focus

The current development phase is centred on benchmarking workflows for policy agenda annotation using multilingual parliamentary text. The goal is to support systematic comparison across LLMs and fine-tuned classifiers under shared evaluation conditions.

At present, the project focuses in particular on:

  • silver-standard benchmark construction;
  • multilingual parliamentary speech data;
  • standardised evaluation pipelines;
  • reproducible model submissions and outputs.

Why this matters

Benchmarking in political text analysis is often fragmented across datasets, prompts, modelling choices, and reporting formats. This project aims to provide common infrastructure for more rigorous comparison. In doing so, it supports OCPSG’s wider mission of fostering computational political science research, training, and policy-relevant methodological innovation.

Explore the project

You can use this site to:

  • learn about the benchmark and its scope;
  • access documentation for workflows and submissions;
  • browse project updates and releases;
  • navigate to the relevant repositories and supporting materials.

Institutional context

The Oxford Computational Political Science Group was founded in December 2024 and has grown as an international network applying computational methods to major political and policy questions. Its mission includes strengthening interdisciplinary research, training the next generation of computational talent, and translating technical work into socially relevant and policy-relevant outputs.

For broader information about the group, see the official Oxford Computational Political Science Group page at Oxford. General enquiries to OCPSG can be directed to ocpsg@politics.ox.ac.uk.
